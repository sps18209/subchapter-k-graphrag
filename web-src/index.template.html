How it works

four layers
statute / authority & basis increase removed / revoked & basis decrease in force / verified
Layer 0 — currency gate

Date-aware

Validity intervals plus typed supersedes edges. As of a transaction date, stale, removed, and revoked authority is hard-blocked. This caught the build's own seed going stale on §199A.

Layer 1 — structural graph

Definition-centric

Terms are hubs; statutes, regs, rulings, and cases attach by typed edges. A computed term like outside basis is a hub whose inbound edges are the formula.

Layer 3 — retrieval

Hybrid + routed

Lexical seed (BM25) → graph expansion → authority-tier rerank with seeds preserved → currency filter. Basis questions are routed to the engine, not reasoned out of text.

Posture

Seed, not ground

Every node is unverified seed for attorney review. Recent items were checked against IRS / Federal Register sources and tagged verified, superseded, reported, or context before encoding.

The calculator is not graph data. The graph reasons about authority; the engine computes numbers. They are decoupled on purpose, because language models mis-order the basis waterfall and drop the floor. The engine self-validates against the two IRS LB&I worked examples.

This page runs the same engine as the Python system, entirely in your browser — the corpus is exported from it, and the retrieval, calculator, and currency logic are ported and checked to parity.

Demonstration only. Not legal or tax advice, and not a substitute for primary-source research or professional judgment. The corpus is unverified seed; verify every citation against controlling authority before relying on it.
